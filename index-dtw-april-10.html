A New Generation of Mega-Marches:
Immigrants Rise Up! May 1st 2017

Only one year ago, the Center for Community Change and the immigrant rights movement celebrated the 10th year anniversary of the May 1st mega-marches of 2006. That day was filled with so much hope for that, together, we could create permanent and positive changes to immigrant families.

Last May 1st, we reflected on the “Immigrant Spring” of 2006 when boycotts and massive demonstrations erupted in response to the infamous “Sensenbrenner Bill” – a set of draconian immigration enforcement measures that passed the House and were later defeated in the Senate. The protests culminated in the marches of May 1st, called A Day Without Immigrants, in which millions of immigrants and their allies flooded the streets in cities across the country ...

In Case You Missed It
Know Your Rights
With help from CCC, FIRM group CHIRLA posted a chilling and impactful portrayal of what immigrants are facing on a daily basis from ICE and in detention in a new Know Your Rights video, America, Yo Tambien.
The video was created by former CHIRLA communications staff, Anike Tourse and Executive Director Angelica Salas.  It features Barkhad Abdi from the film Captain Phillips, as well as members of CHIRLA and partner groups like the Korean Resource Center.  It has subtitles in Spanish, Korean and Chinese.

From Our Writing Fellow
Thomas Kennedy, who came to the U.S. as a child with his family and was raised here as an undocumented immigrant, wrote a piece that looks how a lack of health care when they need it the most rips families apart. In a piece published on the Huffington Post, he focuses on how the GOP is attempting to conduct highway robbery on the American people and leave 24 million Americans without healthcare. As we know, raising the voices of impacted people such as Thomas had a huge effect on how we succeeded in sinking the Republican Party’s efforts to repeal the Affordable Care Act.

Holocaust Survivor Bernard Marks
Bernard Marks, 87, has gone viral in a video shared hundreds of thousands of times on Facebook and Twitter in which he confronts a top U.S. immigration law enforcement official and the Sacramento County Sheriff at a town hall. “History is not on your side,” he told officials. Marks sent chills through the town hall as he told people that he was 8 when he and his Jewish family were sent from their home to a concentration camp. His father changed his birth year to make him seem older after he learned that the Nazis were sending children under the age of 10 to gas chambers ...

Putting Families First Advisory Council
CCC’s Putting Families First Advisory Council member Manuel Pastor was quoted in the New York Times for a story entitled Immigrant Shock: Can California Predict the Nation’s Future. In the piece, Pastor, a USC sociologist, recounts California’s history with immigration and explains how the demographic change in California between 1980 and 2000 mirrors the change (and projected change) in the United States since 2000 and up to 2050, when white are expected to be less than half of the nation’s population ...
